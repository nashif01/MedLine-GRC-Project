01. ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (AUP)
===========================================================

PURPOSE:
The Acceptable Use Policy ensures all MedLine workforce members use systems, networks, devices, applications, and PHI appropriately, securely, and in compliance with HIPAA.

SCOPE:
Applies to all employees, contractors, temps, volunteers, interns, and third parties with access to MedLine systems, networks, data, or facilities.

DEFINITIONS:
PHI: Protected Health Information  
Authorized Use: Actions required for job duties  
System: Any MedLine-owned device, network, cloud service, or application

REQUIREMENTS:
- Systems used only for authorized job duties.  
- PHI confidentiality must be maintained at all times.  
- Passwords cannot be shared with anyone.  
- Only authorized software may be installed.  
- Attempts to bypass any security control are treated as intrusions.  
- Personal devices prohibited from personnel networks.  
- All PHI must be protected; transmission must be encrypted.  
- MFA required for all accounts.  
- Screens lock after 15 seconds of inactivity.  
- Use only approved clinic WiFi for business purposes.  
- PHI cannot be emailed, texted, or transmitted via unencrypted channels.

ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Workforce must follow all usage rules.  
IT enforces controls.  
Compliance monitors HIPAA adherence.  
Leadership ensures enforcement.

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT:
Violations result in disciplinary action up to termination and possible HIPAA penalties.

EXCEPTIONS:
Only with written approval from Executive Leadership & Compliance.

REVISION HISTORY:
Version 1.0 — Initial Release.

02. PASSWORD & AUTHENTICATION POLICY
===========================================================

PURPOSE:
To ensure strong authentication controls that prevent unauthorized access to PHI and critical systems.

SCOPE:
Applies to all MedLine users, systems, applications, and administrators.

DEFINITIONS:
MFA: Multi-Factor Authentication  
Privileged User: Elevated access account  
Lockout: Automatic account disablement after failed attempts

REQUIREMENTS:
- Complex passwords required; minimum 12 characters.  
- Must include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols.  
- Password rotation every 60 days; privileged accounts every 30 days.  
- MFA required for all users.  
- Accounts lock after 2 failed attempts.  
- No shared accounts permitted.  
- Access removed immediately upon termination.

ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Users maintain password secrecy.  
IT enforces authentication controls.  
Compliance ensures HIPAA alignment.

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT:
Failure to comply may result in corrective action, loss of access, or termination.

EXCEPTIONS:
Only with Compliance approval.

REVISION HISTORY:
Version 1.0.

03. ACCESS CONTROL POLICY
===========================================================

PURPOSE:
To restrict access to PHI and critical systems using Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) and HIPAA Minimum Necessary Standard.

SCOPE:
All users, devices, systems, networks, and vendors.

DEFINITIONS:
Minimum Necessary Standard: HIPAA requirement limiting PHI access.  
RBAC: Role-Based Access Control.

REQUIREMENTS:
- Access must align with job duties.  
- Minimum necessary enforced at all times.  
- Privileged access requires IT + Compliance approval.  
- Quarterly access reviews required.  
- Immediate removal of access upon termination or reassignment.  
- Shared accounts prohibited.  
- Vendors given strictly limited access.

ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Workforce uses only authorized access.  
IT manages access provisioning.  
Compliance oversees HIPAA alignment.  
Leadership supports enforcement.

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT:
Violations result in disciplinary measures.

EXCEPTIONS:
Only with Executive + Compliance approval.

REVISION HISTORY:
Version 1.0.

04. INCIDENT RESPONSE POLICY (IR)
===========================================================

PURPOSE:
To detect, respond to, contain, eradicate, and recover from security incidents, ensuring PHI protection and HIPAA compliance.

SCOPE:
All PHI, systems, networks, cloud platforms, internal servers, and personnel.

DEFINITIONS:
Security Incident: Unauthorized access or threat.  
Breach: Unauthorized PHI disclosure.  
IR Team: Group handling incidents.  
Containment/Eradication/Recovery: NIST IR lifecycles.

REQUIREMENTS:
- Staff must report incidents immediately.  
- IT validates alerts and investigates.  
- IR Team leads containment and recovery.  
- HIPAA breach evaluation mandatory.  
- Full documentation required for all incidents.  
- NIST IR Lifecycle followed (Prep, ID, Contain, Eradicate, Recover, Lessons Learned).  
- No tampering with logs or evidence.  
- Only approved communication channels permitted.

ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Workforce: Report incidents immediately.  
IT: Investigate, contain, recover.  
Compliance: Breach determination + regulatory reporting.  
Leadership: Oversight and escalation.  
Vendors: Must report incidents involving MedLine data.

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT:
Failure to report incidents results in disciplinary action up to termination.

EXCEPTIONS:
Only with Executive + Compliance approval.

REVISION HISTORY:
Version 1.0.

05. DATA PROTECTION & ENCRYPTION POLICY
===========================================================

PURPOSE:
To ensure PHI and sensitive data are encrypted, protected, and handled securely per HIPAA Security Rule.

SCOPE:
All systems, personnel, devices, cloud platforms, backups, and PHI-handling processes.

DEFINITIONS:
Encryption at Rest / In Transit  
Minimum Necessary Standard  
Data Breach  
Data Custodian  
De-identification

REQUIREMENTS:
- PHI encrypted at rest and in transit using FIPS 140-2.  
- No PHI may be stored locally without approval.  
- Only approved HIPAA-compliant cloud environments allowed.  
- Secure deletion required.  
- PHI cannot be transmitted through unapproved channels.  
- Logs must be protected, monitored, tamper-evident.  
- Vendors must follow encryption standards.

ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Workforce: Protect PHI.  
IT: Enforce encryption controls.  
Compliance: Ensure HIPAA alignment.  
Leadership: Approve exceptions.  
Vendors: Demonstrate compliance.

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT:
Violations may lead to corrective action, HIPAA penalties, or access revocation.

EXCEPTIONS:
Only with Leadership + Compliance approval, must remain HIPAA-compliant.

REVISION HISTORY:
Version 1.0.

06. VENDOR RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY (VRM)
===========================================================

PURPOSE:
To ensure vendors handling PHI are secure, HIPAA-compliant, assessed, vetted, monitored, and contractually obligated.

SCOPE:
All vendors, contractors, cloud platforms, MSPs, consultants, and any outside party with system or data access.

DEFINITIONS:
Vendor  
Business Associate (BA)  
BAA  
Vendor Risk Assessment  
Offboarding  
Critical Vendor

REQUIREMENTS:
- Vendors must sign a BAA before handling PHI.  
- Mandatory security assessment before onboarding.  
- Minimum necessary access enforced.  
- Continuous monitoring of vendor activity.  
- Annual vendor security reviews.  
- Vendors must immediately report breaches.  
- Unauthorized subcontracting prohibited.  
- Upon termination, vendors must securely delete or return all data.

ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Workforce: Report unapproved vendor activity.  
Managers: Ensure proper vendor onboarding.  
IT: Assess vendor systems + monitor access.  
Compliance: Enforce HIPAA vendor requirements.  
Leadership: Authorize high-risk vendors.  
Vendors: Follow all controls and reporting obligations.

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT:
Immediate termination for:
- Security noncompliance  
- Breach concealment  

EXCEPTIONS:
Emergency clinical exceptions only, must remain HIPAA-compliant.

REVISION HISTORY:
Version 1.0.

07. BACKUP & DISASTER RECOVERY (BDR) POLICY
===========================================================

PURPOSE:
To ensure MedLine can maintain continuity of care and restore systems rapidly after outages, cyberattacks, or disasters.

SCOPE:
All PHI, systems, networks, EHR, backups, DR sites, IT staff, and vendors.

DEFINITIONS:
Backup  
Disaster Recovery  
RTO  
RPO  
Failover  
Replication  
Backup Integrity

REQUIREMENTS:
- Daily encrypted backups required.  
- Onsite + cloud + offsite redundancy mandatory.  
- Weekly disaster recovery (DR) testing required.  
- RTO = 4 hours for critical systems.  
- RPO = 24 hours.  
- Logs + configurations backed up twice weekly.  
- Restoration must follow documented process.  
- Only IT may perform restorations.  
- Backup integrity verified regularly.

ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Workforce: Report outages.  
IT: Maintain backups, perform DR testing, restore systems.  
Compliance: Audit and ensure HIPAA availability compliance.  
Leadership: Ensure resources for DR.  
Vendors: Must meet DR obligations.

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT:
Zero tolerance for:
- Backup tampering  
- Missed backups  
- Backup corruption  
- Failure to report DR failures  

EXCEPTIONS:
None permitted.

REVISION HISTORY:
Version 1.0.
