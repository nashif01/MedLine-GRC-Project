===============================================
        MEDLINE URGENT CARE CLINIC
        RISK ASSESSMENT REPORT 
===============================================
------------------------------------------------
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------
MedLine Urgent Care Clinic faces significant cybersecurity,
operational, and HIPAA compliance risks. Five HIGH-risk
findings were identified that require immediate attention.
These risks affect PHI confidentiality, patient safety,
operational continuity, and regulatory compliance.

------------------------------------------------
2. SCOPE & METHODOLOGY
------------------------------------------------
Scope Includes:
- IT systems, cloud services, IoT medical devices
- Personnel & human factors
- Physical security
- Administrative processes
- Third-party vendors

Methodology:
- Asset Identification
- Threat Identification
- Vulnerability Analysis
- HIPAA Qualitative Risk Scoring (Low/Moderate/High)
- Final Risk Register
- Recommended Mitigations

------------------------------------------------
3. ASSET INVENTORY
------------------------------------------------
Information Assets:
- Patient PHI
- Billing & Insurance Data
- Lab Results & Diagnostics
- Employee HR Records
- Patient Payment Info

Technical Assets:
- Cloud EHR System
- Local File Server
- Laptops & Workstations
- Medical IoT Devices
- Clinic Wi-Fi (Staff + Guest)
- Microsoft 365 Environment

Physical Assets:
- Clinic Building
- Exam Rooms
- Filing Cabinets (Paper PHI)
- Server Closet
- Network Hardware

Personnel Assets:
- Doctors, Nurses, Medical Assistants
- Receptionists
- Cleaning Staff
- Clinic Manager / Security Officer

------------------------------------------------
4. THREAT REGISTER (SUMMARY)
------------------------------------------------
Key Threats:
- Phishing & Social Engineering
- Ransomware Attacks
- MITM Attacks on Wi-Fi
- Unauthorized Access
- Vendor Breaches
- System Outages
- Insider Misuse
- Physical Theft

------------------------------------------------
5. VULNERABILITY REGISTER (SUMMARY)
------------------------------------------------
Major Vulnerabilities:
- Outdated policies & no risk register
- Weak IAM & no MFA
- Outdated firewalls & unpatched servers
- Flat network architecture
- No disk encryption
- Minimal logging & no alerting
- Weak physical access controls
- Outdated & untested backups
- No vendor risk assessments
- Lack of security training

------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------
Likelihood: Low / Moderate / High
Impact:    Low / Moderate / High

------------------------------------------------
7. FINAL RISK REGISTER (HIGH PRIORITY RISKS)
------------------------------------------------
1. Social Engineering + No Training
   Likelihood: Moderate
   Impact: High
   FINAL RISK: HIGH

2. MITM Attack via Improper Wi-Fi Segmentation
   Likelihood: High
   Impact: High
   FINAL RISK: HIGH

3. Ransomware + Outdated Firewalls
   Likelihood: Moderate
   Impact: High
   FINAL RISK: HIGH

4. Backup Failure + Outdated Backups
   Likelihood: High
   Impact: High
   FINAL RISK: HIGH

5. IoT Devices on Flat Network
   Likelihood: High
   Impact: High
   FINAL RISK: HIGH

------------------------------------------------
8. MITIGATION RECOMMENDATIONS
------------------------------------------------
High-Priority Controls:
- Enforce MFA for all systems
- Implement mandatory phishing/security training
- Replace outdated firewall hardware; update firmware
- Segment network (IoT VLAN, Staff VLAN, Guest VLAN)
- Enable full disk encryption on all endpoints
- Test backups quarterly; reduce RPO to daily/weekly
- Establish vendor risk management workflow + BAAs
- Deploy endpoint protection/EDR

------------------------------------------------
9. CONCLUSION
------------------------------------------------
MedLine Urgent Care Clinic must address critical risks
related to PHI exposure, outdated systems, lack of training,
and weak network architecture. Implementing the recommended
controls will significantly reduce regulatory, operational,
and cybersecurity risks while improving overall resilience.
